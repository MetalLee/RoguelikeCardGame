# Combat Rework Design: Three-Beat Disarm, Color-Energy Success, Finisher Cut-In

Date: 2026-06-20  
Status: Draft for user review  
Source notes: [[inspiration/2026-06-20_combat_identity_and_animation_brainstorm_qa|战斗特色化与动画爽感头脑风暴 Q&A]]

## Purpose

This design reworks the current card combat direction so that 《剑与黑塔》 is no longer primarily a traditional “player turn plays cards, enemy turn resolves intent” deckbuilder. The new combat identity is:

> Three-beat disarm + success-generated color energy + finisher comic cut-in.

The first implementation slice intentionally does **not** include color enchantment or five-color finisher conversion. It validates the new battle rhythm first: reading enemy actions, assigning player beats to intercept them, generating color energy from successful actions, and manually cutting in finishers during combat.

## Design Goals

- Differentiate from 《杀戮尖塔》 by replacing long player turns and action-point sequencing with beat-based action choreography.
- Differentiate from 《废墟图书馆》 by making the core reward of clashes “successful action generates color energy, enabling manual finisher cut-ins,” rather than only winning action collisions.
- Make combat feel like a black-and-white comic page driven by player card choices: block, dodge, counter, expose an opening, then cut into a finisher panel.
- Keep the first MVP slice small enough to verify readability and fun before reintroducing color enchantment.

## Core Loop

Each combat round has four stages:

1. **Telegraph**
   Enemies reveal their action sequences for the round. Normal enemies fully reveal their actions. Elite and Boss designs may later hide specific beats through explicit skills or passives.

2. **Choreograph**
   The player assigns action cards into beat slots. Initial weapons, revolver sword and mechanical arm, both provide 3 beats. One action card fits in one beat. Empty beats are allowed. The player may also place one finisher card into the finisher slot.

3. **Resolve**
   Each player beat targets one unrepeated enemy beat. The targeted player action and enemy action collide. Enemy beats that are not targeted still resolve in the enemy's original order. If all beats of an enemy are targeted, remaining player beats may target that enemy's body directly.

4. **Cleanup**
   Resolved action cards enter the discard pile. The finisher slot discards at round end by default. Color energy clears at round end by default. Special effects may preserve the finisher slot or some color energy.

## Cards And Actions

Player-visible card types remain:

- **Action cards**: placed into beat slots.
- **Finisher cards**: placed into the finisher slot.

An action card is a container for one or more actions. One beat contains one card, but that card may contain multiple ordered actions.

Attack actions:

- Slash
- Strike
- Projectile

Non-attack actions:

- Block
- Dodge

Examples:

- `Block 4 -> Strike 6`
- `Dodge 50% -> Slash 3 x2`

Enemy beats are also represented as card-like action blocks, but enemies do not use decks, hands, draw piles, or discard piles. Their action cards are generated by encounter data and AI behavior.

## Collision Rules

When a player beat targets an enemy beat, their action sequences resolve in order:

- Attack vs attack: both sides apply attack damage, creating a clash animation.
- Attack vs block: attack value minus block value becomes remaining damage. This works symmetrically for enemy block against player attack.
- Attack vs dodge: dodge has a base 50% chance to avoid damage entirely. Weapons, relics, cards, or statuses may modify this chance.
- Block/dodge vs block/dodge: both actions are consumed with no damage.
- If one side runs out of actions, the other side's remaining actions apply directly.

When a player beat directly targets an enemy body, only attack actions on that action card resolve. Block and dodge actions on that card are skipped.

## Enemy Resistance

Enemies have three resistance grades for each attack type:

- **Resist**: 50% damage.
- **Standard**: 100% damage.
- **Weakness**: 150% damage.

Slash, strike, and projectile damage all use this resistance table. Finisher attack damage also uses attack type resistance.

Enemy resistance is shown on enemy hover. During drag or targeting, the UI should temporarily show whether the current attack type is resisted, standard, or a weakness against the selected target.

## Color Energy

In the first rework MVP, all color energy is colorless. Color enchantment and five-color conversion are delayed.

Color energy is generated only by successful actions:

- Dealing damage with an attack action.
- Reducing incoming damage with a block action.
- Successfully dodging an attack action.

Each successful action generates 1 colorless energy. The base color energy cap remains 6. If energy is full, later successful actions may waste generation unless an effect expands the cap or spends energy through a finisher.

Color energy clears at round end by default. Preserving energy is a high-value effect reserved for explicit cards, relics, statuses, weapon effects, or finisher-slot rules.

## Finisher Slot

Finishers are no longer played as ordinary beat cards.

- During choreography, the player may place one finisher card into the finisher slot.
- The finisher does not occupy a beat.
- After any successful player action, if current color energy satisfies the finisher's condition, the finisher slot becomes releasable.
- The player may manually click the finisher to cut into a comic-panel finisher animation.
- Releasing the finisher consumes the energy required by that finisher.
- The same finisher can be released multiple times in a round if the player repeatedly regenerates enough energy to meet its condition again.
- Releasing a finisher does not consume the finisher card.
- The finisher slot discards at round end by default.
- Special effects may preserve the finisher slot across rounds.

The UI should avoid modal interruptions. A releasable finisher should use highlight, pulse, brief hit-stop, or slow-motion affordance rather than forcing a popup.

## Hand And Deck Flow

- At combat start, draw 5 cards.
- Hands persist across rounds by default.
- At each round start, draw 2 cards.
- Action cards used in resolved beats enter the discard pile.
- Empty beats are allowed.
- If the draw pile is insufficient, standard reshuffle from discard may be used unless later rules override it.

Action points are not used in the first rework MVP. Beat count, hand availability, card action content, enemy pressure, and finisher energy conditions are the main constraints.

## Multi-Enemy Targeting

Each player beat chooses one target:

- An unrepeated beat from any enemy, or
- An enemy body, only after all of that enemy's beats are already targeted.

Base rules:

- One enemy beat can be targeted by at most one player beat.
- The player may target any beat index from any enemy, not only the matching beat number.
- Untargeted enemy beats still resolve in the enemy's original order.
- If an enemy has all beats targeted, remaining player beats may attack its body directly.

This creates the tactical pattern: disarm dangerous beats first, then punish exposed bodies.

## Enemy Action Cards As Rewards

Enemy action cards are not part of enemy decks, but they are content assets. Later, defeated enemies may offer some of their action cards as player rewards.

If an enemy card enters the player deck:

- It becomes an action card.
- It may be placed into beat slots.
- It follows the same success-based energy generation rules.
- It can later receive color enchantment when that system returns.

The first rework MVP should reserve data fields for card source, but does not need to implement enemy-card rewards.

## UI And Animation

The battle UI should present combat as a three-beat comic page:

- Player side: three beat slots.
- Enemy side: each enemy shows a revealed action sequence.
- Dragging a player card to a beat then targets an enemy beat or exposed enemy body.
- Locked enemy beats show an intercepted state.
- Untargeted enemy beats show danger.
- Enemies whose beats are fully targeted show an exposed state.
- Action cards display their internal action chain.
- Color energy remains a 6-slot meter.
- The finisher slot sits near the energy meter.

Animation principles:

- Ordinary beats animate as block, dodge, clash, counter, or punish moments.
- Successful actions send energy feedback into the energy meter.
- Finishers cut into larger comic panels with speed lines, close-up framing, hit-stop, and clear damage/energy feedback.
- Animation must not obscure unresolved enemy beats, health, energy, or the finisher slot.

## First Rework MVP Scope

Included:

- Player character: 左.
- Weapons: revolver sword and mechanical arm.
- Both initial weapons use 3 beats.
- No action points.
- Combat start draw 5.
- Round start draw 2.
- Persistent hand.
- Action cards and finisher cards only.
- Action types: slash, strike, projectile, block, dodge.
- Enemy resistance: resist / standard / weakness.
- Colorless energy cap 6.
- Successful action generates 1 colorless energy.
- One finisher slot.
- Finisher release after successful actions when energy condition is met.
- Normal enemies fully reveal action sequences.
- Multi-enemy support may start with up to 2 enemies.

Excluded from the first rework MVP:

- Color shards.
- Action-card color enchantment.
- Five-color finisher conversion.
- Hidden enemy beats.
- Enemy-card rewards.
- Full six-fight run replacement.
- More than one finisher slot.
- Action points or replacement cost resources.

Recommended validation slice:

- One tutorial fight teaching beats, targeting, block, attack, and energy generation.
- One two-enemy fight teaching disarm targeting and exposed-body attacks.
- One elite/Boss concept fight teaching finisher timing and resistance targeting.

## Data Model Implications

Action cards need structured action lists:

- Action kind: slash, strike, projectile, block, dodge.
- Value.
- Hit count or repeat count.
- Targeting rules.
- Optional success-trigger effects.

Enemies need action sequences per round or behavior state:

- Beat index.
- Enemy action card ID.
- Reveal state.
- Optional special rule.

Finishers need:

- Energy requirement.
- Attack type, if dealing attack damage.
- Effect payload.
- Whether release timing has special restrictions.
- Whether the finisher slot is preserved by the card or by another effect.

The first MVP may keep color fields dormant. Later color enchantment should attach to the whole action card, not individual actions.

## Risks

- **UI overload**: beat slots, enemy action cards, targeting lines, action chains, resistance, energy, and finishers all compete for attention.
- **Balance spikes**: unlimited finisher release per round may overpower ordinary actions if energy generation is too easy.
- **Dodge variance**: 50% dodge can feel unfair without clear odds and strong feedback.
- **Implementation scope**: this is a combat-loop rewrite, not a small iteration on existing card play.
- **Competitor comparison**: action collision invites comparison to 《废墟图书馆》. The design must emphasize disarming arbitrary enemy beats, success-generated energy, and manual finisher cut-ins.

## Open Questions For Later

- Exact naming for English and Chinese UI terms for beat, disarm, exposed, finisher slot, and successful action.
- Whether enemies can have beat counts different from the player.
- Whether elite/Boss hidden beats are shown as question marks, silhouettes, or false telegraphs.
- How color enchantment returns after the first MVP.
- Whether enemy-card rewards enter normal rewards, rare rewards, or a separate post-fight reward track.
- Whether any specific finisher should have a per-round release cap as a card-specific exception.
